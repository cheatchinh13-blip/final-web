Angkor Wat is the largest religious monument in the world and one of the most important cultural landmarks in Cambodia. Located in Siem Reap, it was originally built in the early 12th century as a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu by King Suryavarman II. Spanning over 400 acres, it is surrounded by a moat that stretches nearly 5 kilometres — an engineering marvel of its time.

The temple is celebrated for its grand architecture, breathtaking bas-reliefs, and spectacular sunrise views. Every surface tells a story — from epic mythological battles to the daily life of the Khmer people. Today, Angkor Wat is a symbol of national pride and prominently features on the Cambodian flag, the only country in the world whose flag depicts a building.

Visitors from all over the world come to explore its layered history, admire its beauty, and absorb the peaceful atmosphere surrounding the entire Angkor Archaeological Park — a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1992. Beyond Angkor Wat itself, the park contains hundreds of temples including Bayon, Ta Prohm, and Banteay Srei.

The best time to visit is during the dry season between November and March, when skies are clear for that iconic sunrise photograph with the temple's five towers perfectly reflected in the still waters of the western reflecting pool.
